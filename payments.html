Section 1

What's being compared.

Both options are credible. They solve overlapping problems with different architectures, and they fit different merchant profiles. This document maps each one against NGS's confirmed requirements.

Shopify Payments

Shopify's native payment processor, integrated directly into the Shopify checkout, admin, and B2B feature set. Used by the majority of Shopify Plus merchants. Available in the US for credit, debit, ACH (via Stripe processor), and digital wallets.

Run Payments

A payment orchestration platform and ISO, registered with Wells Fargo and Fifth Third Bank. Routes transactions through third-party processors (Payroc, TSYS, Fiserv) and gateways (Worldnet, CyberSource, CardPointe, Accept Blue, iStream). Supports embedded payments via API/SDK and is positioned for ISVs, ISOs, and enterprise merchants needing multi-processor flexibility.

Confirmed by Run (June 2026): If NGS uses Run on the new site, the engagement would be a straight gateway model — not aggregated / PayFac. NGS holds its own merchant account directly with the sponsor bank and is underwritten as the merchant of record.

NGS context for this comparison

  • Annual GMV — ~$12M across NGS + Intek
  • Order mix — 60% B2B, 40% D2C, 300–400 orders/day
  • Quote workflow — 30–35% of orders involve a quote before conversion
  • Customer base — 10,000+ customers, 4-tier B2B pricing structure
  • Plan — Shopify Plus (single Organization for NGS + Intek)
  • Go-live — November 2026 soft launch; broader relaunch early 2027

How to read this

Shopify Plus charges a 0.20% third-party transaction fee on transactions processed through any non-Shopify gateway. On NGS's ~$12M GMV that's approximately $24,000/year on top of whatever Run charges. The fee is waived when Shopify Payments is the primary gateway.

Run's interchange-plus model can be advantageous on volume mixes with significant commercial / purchasing card activity (see Section 4). The actual cost comparison depends on three numbers NGS should request in writing: Run's markup over interchange, Shopify's quoted Plus processing rate, and NGS's card mix.

Section 4

Where each provider is strongest.

Both options have legitimate strengths. The question is which set of strengths matters more for NGS's specific volume mix and operating model.

Available only with Shopify Payments

The D2C growth stack NGS forfeits without Shopify Payments.

40% of NGS's revenue (~$4.8M/yr) is D2C. The tools below are not payment features — they're a network of acquisition, conversion, and retention products gated behind Shopify Payments. A third-party gateway like Run can match Shopify on processing, but none of these tools are available without Shop Pay as the primary checkout.

Conversion

Shop Pay accelerated checkout (the purple button)

The fastest checkout on the internet — ~5% measured lower-funnel conversion lift (Forrester Wave Q2 2024). Directionally ~$240K/yr in incremental converted revenue on NGS's D2C side at flat traffic.

Acquisition

Shop App marketplace + product discoverability

NGS's catalog surfaces inside the Shop App to 100M+ active Shop users. Built-in re-engagement, order tracking, and follow-up — a no-cost acquisition and retention channel that exists only for Shopify Payments stores.

Checkout UX

Network checkout autofill

Anyone who's ever used Shop Pay anywhere on the internet sees their address, card, and contact info one-tap loaded on NGS's checkout. Removes friction for first-time D2C buyers — the segment most sensitive to checkout abandonment.

Retention

Shop Cash rewards

Buyer-incentive program funded by Shopify. Customers earn Shop Cash and redeem it across the Shop network. Drives repeat purchase and steers consideration toward Shopify Payments merchants. Cannot be issued by Run.

AOV

Shop Pay Installments

BNPL via Affirm, native at checkout. Lifts AOV on higher-ticket D2C purchases — relevant for $500–$2,000 home gym and replacement-part orders. Only enabled when Shopify Payments is the gateway.

Performance Marketing

Shop Campaigns + Shopify Audiences

CPA-based paid acquisition where Shopify only charges per converted order. Audience matching across the Shop graph to lower paid-ad CAC. Both require Shopify Payments as the gateway.

Trust

One-tap recognized checkout

D2C buyers already trust the Shop Pay UX from millions of other stores. Removes the friction of entering payment details on an unfamiliar brand — a meaningful conversion variable for net-new buyers from paid traffic.

Fraud

Shopify Protect

Chargeback protection on eligible D2C orders, included for stores on Shopify Payments. Eliminates the operational lift of fighting chargebacks on consumer card-not-present transactions.

Shopify Payments — strengths

  • Native B2B feature set Vaulted cards on Company Location, net terms tied to catalog tier, deposits, store credit, PO capture — all delivered out of the box with Plus B2B.
  • Unified ledger One payout, one Shopify Finance view, one tax engine across NGS + Intek + future channels.
  • No third-party transaction fee On Plus, the 0.20% 3P fee is waived when Shopify Payments is the primary gateway.
  • Shop Pay conversion lift ~5% measured lower-funnel conversion uplift for stores using Shop Pay (Forrester Wave Q2 2024).
  • Built-in to checkout No redirect, no separate vault, no second portal for finance to reconcile against Shopify orders.

Run Payments — strengths

  • Level 2 / Level 3 data capture Lowers interchange on business, purchasing, and corporate cards by ~50–100 bps. Material for B2B buyers paying with P-cards.
  • Multi-processor / gateway routing Single integration to many processors and gateways. Most valuable when a merchant needs cross-platform payment ops.
  • Surcharging / dual pricing Built-in toggles for surcharge offsetting — relevant if NGS wants to pass card costs to certain buyer segments. Compatibility with Shopify Checkout has not been validated and would need to be confirmed with Run.
  • Embedded payments via SDK API and mobile SDK support for embedding payments outside a standard checkout flow. Not applicable to a Shopify Plus deployment — Shopify Checkout does not support custom payment integrations, so this capability would not be available on the NGS storefront.
  • ERP-native integrations Direct connectors to NetSuite, SAP, Oracle, Sage — useful during the 2027 NetSuite migration.

Two different pricing models — when each one wins

Run Payments operates on an interchange-plus model, where each transaction is billed at the card network's interchange rate plus a fixed markup. Within that model, capturing Level 2/3 data on commercial, purchasing, and corporate cards lowers interchange by roughly 50–100 bps. The savings are real, but they apply only to the share of volume paid with those specific card types — for NGS, that's most likely the subset of B2B volume coming from enterprise gym chains (24 Hour Fitness, LA Fitness) buying through procurement teams.

Shopify Payments uses a blended-rate model — a single negotiated rate applied across all card types. There is no interchange pass-through and no dependency on L2/L3 data capture. The trade-off: a blended rate is generally simpler to forecast and reconcile, removes the operational burden of validating L2/L3 data quality, and gives finance a predictable per-transaction cost that doesn't fluctuate with card-type mix or interchange changes. The interchange-plus model can be cheaper at scale on heavy commercial-card volume; the blended model is more predictable and lower-overhead.

To compare the two accurately, NGS should pull a sample month of card-mix data from the current processor — specifically the share of transactions on business, purchasing, and corporate cards. That single data point determines whether the interchange-plus savings exceed the predictability and operational benefits of the blended rate.

Section 5

Recommendation.

Based on NGS's confirmed requirements and current operating model.

Shopify Payments fits NGS's requirements as defined today.

Of the 9 requirements mapped in Section 2, Shopify Payments is the native solution for 7 of them — including the four that tie directly to NGS's daily operating model: vaulted card on quote conversion, net terms by Company Location, deposits on large orders, and unified ledger across NGS + Intek. Run Payments offers a different pricing model (interchange-plus with L2/L3 capture) that can be advantageous on heavy commercial-card volume, and matches on the rest.

The cost picture, before any rate negotiation, includes a 0.20% third-party transaction fee on Run-routed transactions that doesn't apply to Shopify Payments. On NGS's volume that's approximately $24,000 per year in addition to Run's processing fees.

The strongest case for Run Payments would emerge if NGS's commercial-card share is high enough that Level 2/3 interchange savings exceed the combined cost of the 3P fee, the lost B2B-native features, and the operational overhead of a second reconciliation surface. Pulling card-mix data from the current processor is the single most valuable next step.
