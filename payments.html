Section 1

What's being compared.

Both options are credible. They solve overlapping problems with different architectures, and they fit different merchant profiles. This document maps each one against NGS's confirmed requirements.

Shopify Payments

Shopify's native payment processor, integrated directly into the Shopify checkout, admin, and B2B feature set. Used by the majority of Shopify Plus merchants. Available in the US for credit, debit, ACH (via Stripe processor), and digital wallets.

Run Payments

A payment orchestration platform and ISO, registered with Wells Fargo and Fifth Third Bank. Routes transactions through third-party processors (Payroc, TSYS, Fiserv) and gateways (Worldnet, CyberSource, CardPointe, Accept Blue, iStream). Supports embedded payments via API/SDK and is positioned for ISVs, ISOs, and enterprise merchants needing multi-processor flexibility.

NGS context for this comparison

  • Annual GMV — ~$12M across NGS + Intek
  • Order mix — 60% B2B, 40% D2C, 300–400 orders/day
  • Quote workflow — 30–35% of orders involve a quote before conversion
  • Customer base — 10,000+ customers, 4-tier B2B pricing structure
  • Plan — Shopify Plus (single Organization for NGS + Intek)
  • Go-live — November 2026 soft launch; broader relaunch early 2027

How to read this

Shopify Plus charges a 0.15% third-party transaction fee on transactions processed through any non-Shopify gateway. On NGS's ~$12M GMV that's approximately $18,000/year on top of whatever Run charges. The fee is waived when Shopify Payments is the primary gateway.

Run's interchange-plus model can be advantageous on volume mixes with significant commercial / purchasing card activity (see Section 4). The actual cost comparison depends on three numbers NGS should request in writing: Run's markup over interchange, Shopify's Plus Exclusive Rate, and NGS's card mix.

Section 4

Where each provider is strongest.

Both options have legitimate strengths. The question is which set of strengths matters more for NGS's specific volume mix and operating model.

Shopify Payments — strengths

  • Native B2B feature set Vaulted cards on Company Location, net terms tied to catalog tier, deposits, store credit, PO capture — all delivered out of the box with Plus B2B.
  • Unified ledger One payout, one Shopify Finance view, one tax engine across NGS + Intek + future channels.
  • No third-party transaction fee On Plus, the 0.15% 3P fee is waived when Shopify Payments is the primary gateway.
  • Shop Pay conversion lift ~5% measured lower-funnel conversion uplift for stores using Shop Pay (Forrester Wave Q2 2024).
  • Built-in to checkout No redirect, no separate vault, no second portal for finance to reconcile against Shopify orders.

Run Payments — strengths

  • Level 2 / Level 3 data capture Lowers interchange on business, purchasing, and corporate cards by ~50–100 bps. Material for B2B buyers paying with P-cards.
  • Multi-processor / gateway routing Single integration to many processors and gateways. Most valuable when a merchant needs cross-platform payment ops.
  • Surcharging / dual pricing Built-in toggles for surcharge offsetting — relevant if NGS wants to pass card costs to certain buyer segments.
  • Embedded payments via SDK API and mobile SDK support for embedding payments outside a standard checkout flow.
  • ERP-native integrations Direct connectors to NetSuite, SAP, Oracle, Sage — useful during the 2027 NetSuite migration.

Where the L2/L3 advantage matters most

Run's Level 2/3 advantage applies only to transactions paid with commercial / purchasing / corporate cards. For NGS, that's most likely the subset of B2B volume coming from enterprise gym chains (24 Hour Fitness, LA Fitness) buying parts through their procurement teams.

To quantify the advantage accurately, NGS should pull a sample month of card-mix data from the current processor — specifically the share of transactions on business, purchasing, and corporate cards. That single data point converts the L2/L3 argument from directional to dollar-specific.

Section 5

Recommendation.

Based on NGS's confirmed requirements and current operating model.

Shopify Payments fits NGS's requirements as defined today.

Of the 11 requirements mapped in Section 2, Shopify Payments is the native solution for 8 of them — including the four that tie directly to NGS's daily operating model: vaulted card on quote conversion, net terms by Company Location, deposits on large orders, and unified ledger across NGS + Intek. Run Payments delivers two requirements more strongly (L2/L3 data and surcharging) and matches on the rest.

The cost picture, before any rate negotiation, includes a $0.15% third-party transaction fee on Run-routed transactions that doesn't apply to Shopify Payments. On NGS's volume that's approximately $18,000 per year in addition to Run's processing fees.

The strongest case for Run Payments would emerge if NGS's commercial-card share is high enough that Level 2/3 interchange savings exceed the combined cost of the 3P fee, the lost B2B-native features, and the operational overhead of a second reconciliation surface. Pulling card-mix data from the current processor is the single most valuable next step.
