Who They Are

B2B Gym Equipment Parts, Across North America

NGS (founded 1993, acquired by Lomita Capital in 2023) sells over 40,000 replacement parts and accessories to commercial gyms, fitness chains (24 Hour Fitness, LA Fitness), colleges, and home gym owners. Their 10,000+ customer base is 60% B2B and 40% direct consumer — the platform must serve both segments without separate catalog management. Buyers need the exact part for the exact machine; spec-matching is the core of their commerce. Sister brand Intek Strength (~$2M, acquired 2025, 55 SKUs, 3,000+ customers, 80% B2B) is also in scope. NGS processes 300–400 orders per day across web, phone, and PO channels. The plan is a soft launch end of 2026 with tenured customers to validate the experience — followed by a full relaunch in early 2027 with a broader digital marketing strategy.

B2B Primary NGS: 40,000 SKUs · Intek: 55 SKUs NGS: 60% B2B · 40% Consumer Two Sites in Scope
Replatform Drivers
  • Custom .NET platform, 15+ years old — end-of-life, increasingly difficult to maintain and extend
  • Previous $250K+ redesign was soft-launched but immediately rejected by tenured customers — broken parts search, wrong machine associations, non-functional services, and payment failures. Poor execution, not a platform issue.
  • ERP (Everest) is end-of-life — migration to NetSuite planned for 2027
  • New VP of Ecommerce engaged specifically to lead this initiative through completion
Key Requirements
  • Modernize the platform without disrupting established B2B purchasing workflows
  • Rich product specs, OEM cross-reference, schematics — the primary failure point of the prior replatform attempt
  • 4-tier B2B pricing, net terms, multi-buyer company accounts
  • Flat file ERP integration at launch (interim approach) → NetSuite connector in 2027
  • NGS + Intek as separate storefronts under one Shopify Organization

Shopify Delivers

12 requirements from DYODE's technical specification and the May 20 discovery call where Shopify Plus meets or exceeds expectations — natively or with minimal configuration.

  • 1
    FR-ACC + FR-PRC · B2B Accounts & Pricing
    4-Tier Pricing + Company Hierarchy — Native
    NGS requires A/B/C/D customer tiers, per-company price lists, and multi-buyer company accounts with inherited pricing. Shopify B2B handles all of this natively — companies, locations, buyers, and catalogs are included with Plus. Tier pricing syncs from ERP via middleware into Shopify B2B Catalogs. No third-party app, no custom code.
  • 2
    FR-CAT-01–11 · Catalog & Product Data
    Machine Compatibility, OEM Cross-Reference & Schematics via Metaobjects
    The prior NGS replatform failed because product specs were missing. The project requires machine make/model compatibility lists, OEM cross-references, schematic PDFs with clickable callouts, and variant fitment data. NGS's catalog is 40,000 SKUs — the vast majority are simple products, with configurable, bundled, and custom product types isolated to the cable category. Shopify Metaobjects handle the structured data layer natively; the cable configurator is the one bespoke component, scoped as a custom app.
  • 3
    FR-SHIP + FR-PRC-06–09 · Shipping & Discount Logic
    Complex Shipping Calculator + Bulk-Qty Discounts via Shopify Functions
    The shipping calculator is the highest-risk subsystem in scope — dimensional weight, oversize/freight rules, nested-product handling, separately-shipped parts, and customer carrier accounts. Critically, NGS ships from two warehouse locations (LA for electronics repairs, Illinois for general parts) — customers currently see two separate shipping line items and can't order from both locations in one transaction. The new site must consolidate into a single shipping charge. DYODE has already scoped this and treats it as low-hanging fruit. NGS also requires bulk-qty price breaks combinable with tier pricing. Shopify Functions express all of this in version-controlled, upgrade-safe code.
  • 4
    FR-CMS-01–06 · Content & Marketing
    Online Store 2.0 — Marketing Team Self-Serves Without Engineering
    The project is scoped non-headless — the NGS marketing team needs section-based CMS control without engineering dependency. Shopify's Online Store 2.0 is exactly this — drag-and-drop sections, scheduled banners, blog/resource library, SEO controls, and redirect maps for legacy OEM cross-reference URLs.
  • 5
    FR-CRT-06–07 · B2B Checkout
    Net Terms, PO Capture & Deferred CC Auth at Checkout — Native
    NGS requires net terms at checkout, PO number as a required field for net-terms accounts, Resolve for credit decisioning, ACH for high-value orders, and deferred CC authorization for orders under internal review. Net terms and PO capture are native Shopify B2B checkout features. Deferred CC auth is a Shopify Function. Resolve integrates via Shopify's app ecosystem.
  • 6
    FR-QUO-01–05 · Quoting
    Save-as-Quote / Draft Orders — Core B2B Workflow Covered
    NGS requires converting a cart to a quote at any point, manager approval routing, quote expiration with reminders, price-locked quote-to-order conversion, and PDF export. Shopify B2B Draft Orders cover the core of this — cart-to-quote, price locking, and order conversion. Manager approval routing and PDF export are buildable on top with Shopify Flow and a lightweight custom app. Additional configuration is required, built on a strong native foundation.
  • 7
    § 2.3 Multi-Brand Strategy
    NGS + Intek + Future Brands Under One Shopify Organization
    The requirement is NGS and Intek as separate storefronts under one Shopify Organization, with independent themes, pricing, and content — but a shared middleware layer. Adding a third or fourth brand should not require rebuilding the middleware. Shopify Plus expansion stores do exactly this. One admin login, shared team, separate customer-facing experiences.
  • 8
    NFR-SEO-01–04 · SEO
    OEM Cross-Reference URL Preservation — Built-In Redirect Infrastructure
    SEO is a non-negotiable requirement given the long-tail of OEM cross-reference pages that drive organic traffic. Requirements include a 301 redirect map from legacy URLs, structured data (Product, Offer, Breadcrumb) on all PDPs, XML sitemaps per brand, and canonical handling for fitment duplicates. Shopify handles all of this natively — bulk redirects, automatic structured data, sitemaps, and canonical URLs are platform-level features.
  • 9
    NFR-PERF + NFR-REL · Performance & Scale
    300–400 Orders/Day — Shopify Plus Built for This and More
    Site performance is a critical requirement — the prior redesign failed in part due to the current site making 700+ real-time API calls to the ERP on every part lookup, creating extreme lag. The fix: store everything in Shopify (using it as a PIM), eliminating real-time ERP dependency for catalog browsing. The ERP is only called when needed (e.g. shipping calculations). Shopify Plus's infrastructure, CDN, and edge caching handle NGS's 300–400 daily orders at peak with headroom to grow. Plus tier comes with higher API rate limits necessary for the integration surface area. Shopify's 99.99% SLA exceeds the requirement directly.
  • 10
    § 9 Tech Stack · Integrations
    Klaviyo, GA4/GTM, and Gorgias — Best-in-Class Native Integrations
    DYODE's tech stack plan calls for Klaviyo (email/retention), GA4 + GTM (analytics, already in place), Google Ads (UTM/conversion tracking preservation), and Gorgias (helpdesk/chat). Shopify has native, deep integrations with all four. Klaviyo's Shopify integration is its flagship integration. Gorgias was built specifically for Shopify merchants. No custom connectors needed for any of these.
  • 11
    Tony's #1 Priority · Search
    Shopify Search & Discovery — AI-Powered, Serial Number Ready
    Tony identified search as the most critical tech stack component. Key NGS challenges: serial numbers that share the same first 3–4 characters, and part numbers where customers type "O" (letter) instead of "0" (zero) — both causing failed searches. Shopify's native search and discovery platform has evolved significantly (confirmed by Jordan) and is the starting point — it handles AI-powered ranking, synonyms, and fuzzy matching. If needed, third-party solutions like Algolia can be added later with minimal friction. Starting native avoids the cost and complexity Tony experienced with SearchSpring at a previous company.
  • 12
    Future-Proofing · Agentic Commerce
    Universal Commerce Protocol — NGS Catalog Visible to AI Agents
    Jordan confirmed that Shopify helped define the Universal Commerce Protocol alongside OpenAI and Google — the framework that governs how AI agents and LLMs reference commerce data and transact. For NGS, a highly SKU-intensive B2B business that relies heavily on organic discovery, this is a significant advantage: their full catalog becomes automatically visible to AI agents as a feature you simply turn on. No custom implementation required. This positions NGS ahead of competitors in the fitness parts space, none of whom have adopted this yet.

Where custom development is required: The Repair Authorization (RA) module, Cable Configurator, Cash for Course buyback, and Sell Parts bid workflow are bespoke — DYODE's specification identifies these explicitly as custom applications. This is expected for an operation of NGS's complexity. The platform handles all surrounding commerce infrastructure natively.

Integration Strategy

NGS's ERP transition is a two-phase approach — designed to launch cleanly in 2026 without dependency on Everest, then connect to NetSuite when it's ready in 2027.

Phase 1 — Launch (2026)

Flat File Integration

  • Current ERP is Everest — bespoke, end-of-life, not practical to integrate temporarily
  • Confirmed approach: flat file sync between Shopify and Everest at launch — avoids building a throwaway connector
  • Products, pricing, inventory, and orders move via scheduled flat file exchange until NetSuite is live
  • DYODE is scoping the exact file format and sync cadence as part of hours estimation
Phase 2 — 2027

NetSuite Go-Live

  • NGS migrating to NetSuite in 2027, after the Shopify launch
  • Intek migrates to NetSuite first — NGS follows
  • Flat file layer replaced by a proper API connector — no Shopify rebuild required
  • Shopify has an Oracle-built native NetSuite connector available for simpler flows (catalog, customer master)

Confirmed out of scope: No CRM integration, no EDI, no loyalty program, no gift cards, no subscriptions, no international requirements. This significantly reduces integration surface area compared to a typical B2B replatform of this size.

✓ Confirmed May 20: Inventory syncs via flat file every 10–15 minutes — Tony confirmed this frequency is sufficient, as inventory doesn't vary significantly and multiple offline channels share the same pool. Final flat file format and order sync cadence to be confirmed with Brian (IT Lead) in technical scoping.
